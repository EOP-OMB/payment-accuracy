  rac3: In FY 21, the Internal Revenue Service- US Coronavirus Payments was determined
    to be susceptible to significant improper payments. In August 2022 Treasury determined
    that due to the short-term nature of these programs, quantifying the amount and
    rate of improper payments, assessing the root cause, and developing corrective
    action plans to reduce payment errors in the future would provide minimal value
    and be an ineffective use of resources. Therefore, for its COVID-19 pandemic relief
    programs Treasury decided it would not report an improper payment estimate either
    in its Agency Financial Report or on paymentaccuracy.gov. The COVID-19 pandemic
    relief programs will remain on the three-year risk assessment rotation.
